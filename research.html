Active Galactic Nuclei Feedback

Galaxies are known to host super massive black holes (SMBH) at their centre. These are black holes which have masses of the order of millions times the solar mass. But the mass of the black hole itself is seen to be a very very tiny fraction of the mass of the galaxy. But observations show that the mass of the central black hole is correlated with the motion of gas and stars in the farthest reaches of the visible galaxy. This cannot be explained gravitationally and hence the idea of AGN feedback jumps in. It is seen in galaxy clusters that the SMBH is often accreting matter around it and hence is radiating and such a SMBH is called Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN). These AGN ener outputs show significant effect on the gas properties of the clusters. Similar effect is expected and has been observed in galaxy scales. The energy output of the AGN itself can couple with the contents of its host galaxy and hence can influence various properties of the galaxy including the gas content of the galaxy itself. It is believed that the AGN through its feedback can play an important but complex role in the growth and evolution of its host galaxy. Multiple probes to observationally study the feedback mechanism exists. I work on X-ray signatures of AGN feedback on galaxies. The gas falling in the dark matter gravitational potential of the galaxy emits diffuse X-rays. But if the galaxy hosts an AGN, through its feedback mechanism it will push the gas radially outwards. Hence one expects to see a deficit of X-ray emission around AGN compared inactive SMBH hosting galaxies. We have observed this effect in high redshift (0.3-0.6) galaxies using Chandra and DEEP-2 data. Please loo through my Publications page for the relevant paper.
